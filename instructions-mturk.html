Welcome! In this HIT you will use this web page to play a number of games.

HIT overview

You will participate in @@gamelab-rounds@@ rounds. In each round you and others will make decisions in @@gamelab-games@@ games. These games are identical in all rounds.

In addition to the completion fee you will earn a bonus payment that depends on both your own and others' decisions in the games.

Each round will last for at least @@gamelab-round-mintime-minutes@@ minutes and will end when either @@gamelab-round-percent@@% of all participants have played or after @@gamelab-round-time-minutes@@ minutes have passed.

Game setup

Each game is given in a table and you will have to choose one or more rows to play in each. You will then be matched against one of the other participants who will choose one or more columns.

Your payoff for a given row and column combination is the first number in each cell, shown in: . The column player's payoff is the second number in each cell, shown in: . Your payoff contributes to your bonus payment, with higher payoffs resulting in a higher bonus payment.

You will only get payoffs from the rows you choose to play. However, column players will be choosing which of the columns to play, so you wonâ€™t have full control over the payoff you get.

After you have chosen which rows to play you must assign a weight to each of these. The weights must add up to one. A weight of 1 means that payoffs (for you and the column player) will be calculated solely based on that row. A smaller weight, for example 1/3, means that 1/3 of your payoffs will be calculated from that row. The columns from which your payoffs are calculated are chosen by the column player in the same fashion.

To make a decision in a game, first click all rows you want to play and then input the weights you want to assign to them. Once you have chosen rows and weights, click the Submit button and then the Go to next game button to proceed to the next game.

You must make decisions for all @@gamelab-games@@ games in a round in order to receive payoffs from that round.

Bonus payments

Your payoffs from a game depend on the rows and weights you choose, as well as how the column players choose on average. Other players' payoffs are calculated in the same fashion.

At the end of the task your bonus payment will be calculated by summing your payoffs from all games in all rounds in which you have participated. Your payoff will be converted to @@gamelab-currency@@ at a 1 payoff point = @@gamelab-conversion@@ rate.

History of play

The graphs on the play page and on the History tab provide information about the games and how they have been played in previous rounds. This information includes:

  • Your previous payoffs.
  • How much payoff you would have gotten by choosing each row with full weight (1) in previous rounds.
  • The average choices of the row and column players in previous rounds.
