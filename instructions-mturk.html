Game setup

Each game is represented in a table where you always choose row(s). You will be playing with other players, some also choose row(s) and others choose column(s). Your payoff (which contributes to your bonus payment) for a given row and column combination is the first number in each cell, shown in . The column player's payoff is the second number in each cell, shown in .

Being a row player you will have to choose which row(s) to play. You can only get payoffs from the row(s) you play. However, column players will be choosing which column(s) to play so you donâ€™t have full control over which payoff you get.

When choosing row(s) you must give each played row a weight. A weight of 1 means that payoffs (for you and the column player) will always be calculated based on that row. A weight of, for example 1/3 means that 1/3 of the payoffs will be calculated from that row. Any combination of weights is possible as long as the sum of all weights is 1. Remember that the column(s) from which your payoffs are calculated are chosen by the column players.

To choose row(s), click all rows you want to play and then input the weight you want to assign the rows you have selected in their corresponding fields. When you have selected rows and weights, click the Submit button and then the Go to next game button to proceed to the next game.

Bonus payments

Your payoff from a game depends on how you play (which row(s) you choose and the weight you assign to the row(s)) and on how the column players play on average (which column(s) they choose and the weight they assign to the column(s)).

At the end of the HIT your bonus payment will be calculated by summing your payoffs from all games in all rounds and converting the points to US dollars at a 1 point = $XX rate. You must make choices for all games in a round in order to receive bonus payments from that round.

History of play

To get information about a game in previous rounds you can look at the graphs on the play page and on the History tab. The graphs gives you information about things such as:

  • Your previous payoffs.
  • How much payoff you would have gotten by choosing each row with full weight (1) in previous rounds.
  • The average choices of the row and column players in previous rounds.
